About Us


A non-profit company

100% Black Owned

50% Women Empowered

50% Youth Empowered

100% South African

Non-profit with a commercial arm

Located in Newton Park, Gqeberha, with a focus in the greater EC

Learn More
Bukanindalo is a registered South African Non Profit Company (000--NPC) and Public Beneficiary Organisation (PBO 0000 0000), committed to empowering and developing previously disadvanteaged organized groups that focus on making a contribution and change in tourism, arts & culture and environmental sustainability.
We have develope a variety of programmes to uplift a diversity of people – from the most vulnerable to those who have the potential but lack the opportunity to become self supporting. We guide and equip the groups/organizations we work with to become agents of change of themselves, their families and future generations.

We all hold the power to contribute in some way or another to the societies in which we live, and to become part of the solution.

Be part of the Bukanindalo solution by making a donation now towards the cost of running these vital programmes.

Meet Our Team


Our team boasts individuals that have unique and strategic expertise that ensure the succesfully execution of our mission.

Bandile Kenqa

CEO

bandile@bukanindalo.org

Bandile is a well-rounded and an astute businessman who has acquired wealth of experience from the finance industry, having worked for two of the top 5 banks as a regional manager for more than 20 years. After qualifying for his MBA, he then pursued his business interests. He served as a Board member for Makana Resorts, an Managing Executive for Bhotani Group, and Business Consultant for IBI Consulting.

He is recognized for revitalizing organizations in accordance with ever-changing market trends, identifying business and customer requirements, defining value propositions, setting business objectives and implement comprehensive business solutions.

Mike Pantsi

Chairman

mike@bukanindalo.org

Mike is a social entrepreneur and community builder that develops innovative solutions for communities. He is a former Chairman of Nelson Mandela Bay Tourism, and has sat on the board of Eastern Cape Tennis. He is a founding member of Gompo Friends of the Library and IMBEWU SA, an NGO that has partnered with the Roger Federer Foundation and Nike SA.

Mike also brings with him extensive experience in the entertainment industry. He was a film production manager for the late Ben Nomoyi Films, a boxing ring announcer for the Eastern Cape and was also Dr Rebecca Malope's National Music Promotor. He currently serves as the Chairman for Bukanindalo NPC.

Pearl Jonas

Head of Environmental
& Sustainable Development

pearl@bukanindalo.org

Pearl is one of the best Environmental Educators in Nelson Mandela Bay. She has more than 20 years experience in the field. Her passion to bring excellence to service delivery in the Environmental and Sustainable Development platfrom is massive, and dates back to her working with the Wilderness Foundation. From 2008 - 2018 she worked for WESSA as an Eco-School Co-ordinator. Pearl has been very instrumental in bringing the Eco-Schools program to previously disadvantaged communities which saw her relocated to improve schools in KZN and Mpumalanga.

Recently she facilitated a Climate Change community training for Benguela Current Convention. She is the main presenter for the NMB Municipality for their drought and water scarcity programme since 2018. She currently serves as the Head of Environment and Sustainable Development for Bukanindalo NPC.

Lindelwa Kenqa

Head of Skills Developments /
Head of Arts & Culture

lindi@bukanindalo.org

Lindi is a creative entrepreneur, however before embarking on her entrepreneurial journey, she worked in the Financial Industry for 12 years. After qualifying in Interior Design she started an Interior Design & Décor business in Butterworth and East London that she managed up until 2008 that is when she founded Bhotani Group (Accommodation, Hospitality & Tours). It is her extensive experience in Operations, Project Management, Customer service and a strong work ethic that saw the companies she founded grow from strength to strength. Under her headship, the 4-Star graded establishments have earned various accolades in the tourism industry.

A mental health advocate and survivor herself, Lindi has served as deputy chair in the Port Elizabeth Mental Health Board. Her genuine interest in helping others served as motivation to empowering and equipping herself with a counselling qualification before she volunteered her services to HIV/Aids infected and affected individuals and communities. Calling herself a ‘Penniless Philanthropist, she currently volunteers her time and skills to field ministry, social and economic empowerment initiatives as she strongly believes in lifting others as she rises. As a hobby, Lindi upcycles waste and turns it into elegant home artifacts.

Linda Kenqa

Co-founder
& Executive Director

linda@bukanindalo.org

Linda is a Web Developer. He assists organizations reach bigger audiences using a platitude of digital services, and has a passion for building technological solutions that alleviate socio-economic issues. He was a Fibre Specialist for a telecommunications company in Gqeberha, and that's where he began his professional career in the tech industry. Before that, he worked for over 5 years in an operational and logistical capacity within the tourism industry.

Linda uses the infrastructure of technology to advance Bukanindalo's causes, and to increase our social impact. He currently serves as an IT Consultant and Executive Director for Bukanindalo NPC.
